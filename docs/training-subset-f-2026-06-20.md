## Major Discovery 1

Registry Family Recognition Is Emerging

Subset F shows that single registry terms are no longer enough.

Some card names belong to related families.

Example family:

Topps Cosmic Planet / Planetary ecosystem:

- Planet Earth
- Planet Mercury
- Planet Portraits
- Planetary Pursuit
- Planetary Pursuit Sun
- Sun

Future registry should support parent-child relationships.

Example:

Topps Cosmic Registry
-> Planetary Pursuit
-> Sun

## Major Discovery 2

Official Rookie Card Types Must Be Protected

Examples:

- Rated Rookie
- Rookie Threads
- Rookie Materials

These should not be collapsed into generic `RC`.

Important rule:

`RC` can remain as an attribute, but official rookie card type names should also be preserved when visible or strongly supported.

## Major Discovery 3

Registry Recognition Does Not Equal Title Inclusion

The system may know more than it should display.

Example:

`On-Card Auto` is useful, but not always a registry card type.

Future title generation should pass through a commercial importance filter:

Registry Knowledge
-> Commercial Importance Ranking
-> Final Title

## Major Discovery 4

Multi-player Relic Cards Need Better Handling

Dual-subject relic cards require stronger rules for:

- preserving both subjects
- preserving order
- preserving dual relic/patch/auto terminology
- preserving serial number

Examples:

- Dual Patch
- Dual Jersey
- Dual Relic
- Dual Auto
